TITLE: The Mysterious Disappearance of the Roanoke Colony
---
In 1587, a group of English settlers arrived on Roanoke Island, off the coast of what is now North Carolina. Led by John White, they established a colony and began building a new life. But when White returned to England for supplies, he was delayed by war with Spain for three years. When he finally came back in 1590, the colony had vanished without a trace.

The only clue left behind was the word "CROATOAN" carved into a post, and "CRO" scratched into a tree. There were no bodies, no signs of struggle, and no evidence of where the 115 settlers had gone. To this day, the fate of the Roanoke Colony remains one of America's greatest unsolved mysteries.

Some theories suggest they integrated with the local Croatoan tribe. Others propose they were killed by hostile Native Americans or Spanish raiders. But no definitive proof has ever been found, leaving historians and archaeologists still searching for answers more than 400 years later.
